Content (exact, do not edit):

Why charged inquiry works when neutral inquiry doesn't.

Inquiry in a neutral state follows a weak signal. You're looking 
for a self that isn't currently defending anything. The search 
comes up empty — but the nervous system doesn't update, because 
there was no real test. No threat, no activation, nothing at stake. 
You can't discover whether you're bulletproof when no one is shooting.

When the existential alarm is ringing — job gone, relationship over, 
identity on the line — the signal is loud and direct. The nervous 
system is fully committed to protecting something specific. That's 
when the test is real.

Follow the bullets inward. Find the self that's about to be 
destroyed. Discover there's nothing there to receive the hit.

Not blanks. No target.

Run that enough times and no-self stops being a concept the mind 
holds and becomes a confirmed fact the nervous system has tested. 
The update happens at the level where the alarm originates — not 
in the understanding, in the prediction system itself.

That's what the app does. It doesn't create the insight. It waits 
until something is actually charged, removes the routes you'd 
normally take to avoid it, and walks the attention back to what's 
being protected. Direct contact, not understanding. The shift 
happens in the system that was predicting danger — not in the mind 
that was trying to figure it out.

Same discovery. Stronger signal. Repeated until the ground holds.

The app doesn't give you answers. It removes escape long enough 
for what's true to be seen.
