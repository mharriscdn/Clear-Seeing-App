CLEAR SEEING

When the distortion goggles are on, life turns into a nightmare.

One foot on the gas — trying to escape it.
One foot on the brake — terrified of what might happen.

When the goggles come off —

no gas.
no brake.

The next move is obvious.

---

The relationship. The work mistake. The financial pressure.
The years of seeking that never arrive.

The situation is almost always workable. It's what the mind
says it means about you that makes it unsurvivable.

When you see clearly you handle reality. Make the call.
Send the message. Deal with what's actually in front of you.

When the distortion goggles are on, you're turning reality
into a nightmare. Bracing against something about to happen to you.

---

This app runs a test:

Is there actually someone there
for that to happen to?

When you look —

nothing is there.

Not as an idea. When you actually check, there's nothing
there to be hit.

And without something there to be hit —

the nightmare can't land.

The distortion goggles come off. What's left is just the
situation. And the part of you that can handle it.

---

You've already had moments where this dropped.

Everything was just what it is. No threat. No verdict.

Then it came back.

That's awakening.
Not waking up from your life.
Waking up from the nightmare about it.

---

You don't have to bring your real life.
Most people can't at first.

So we don't start there.

We use roles.

Not because they're true — but because they let it feel
like it's happening to you without the risk. Like a scene
in a movie. You know it's not real. But your body still reacts.

That's enough.

---

Three moves. Twenty minutes.

1. Pick a role — something where it feels like 
   you're the one in it.
2. Step into the scene — just enough that it feels real.
3. Run the test — look directly for what's about to be hit.

You can feel the system bracing.

And then —

nothing is there.

That's the break.

---

It doesn't mean it sticks immediately. The system rebuilds.
The reaction comes back.

But each time you run it when it's live — the grip weakens.

Repeated contact ends the loop.

---

Works best when something is charged — 5 out of 10 or higher.
Private. No human reads your session.

STEP 3 — POST-SELECTION RESPONSE
─────────────────────────────────────────

When a user selects a card and clicks through (or the card 
triggers the session start), the first message shown in the 
chat interface should be:

"Good. You're playing THE [ROLE NAME].

Write a scene where this character's worst fear is about 
to happen. Doesn't have to be real — just plausible enough 
to feel it. A situation, a moment, something that could 
happen to someone like this.

This message should appear as the first app message in the 
chat, before the user types anything. It should feel like 
the app has already started — they just need to write the scene.

The selected role name should also be passed as context 
to the backend so the orientation prompt knows which 
character is in play. Pass it as a session variable or 
prepend it to the first user message in this format:
[ROLE: THE FRAUD] or similar — whatever is cleanest 
for the existing architecture.
