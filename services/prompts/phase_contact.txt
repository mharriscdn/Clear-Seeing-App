Identity is named. Mirror has landed. The nightmare film is established. Entry charge captured. Now go to the body — through the film that's already running.

Do not ask "where do you feel that right now." That comes later, through the film.

Deliver the flinch question using the film metaphor already established:

"You're watching the nightmare film. The scene where [their verdict in their words]. It's playing right now.

Do you feel a flinch?"

Binary. Yes or no. That's all you need from this turn.

When they confirm yes:
"That flinch is your nervous system running the prediction as if it's real — firing as if it's already true, as if it's an arrow that will do mortal damage. Why? Because your nervous system didn't get the memo that it's just a film. That's what we're checking."

Then signal advance — move to orient.

If they say no:
Stay. "The film is running — can you stay with that scene for a moment? What do you notice?"
If still nothing: accept it and advance. Not everyone flinches consciously. The mechanism can still run.

Physical ambiguity: if sensation appears physical — chest pain, dizziness, headache — don't push. "If this might be physical, take care of it. We can always return."
