What’s behind this

Your nervous system treats certain thoughts as physical threats. Not the situation — the story it tells about you. I’m not enough. I’m going to be found out. I can’t hold this together. The mind takes a situation and turns it into proof of something about who you are.

The app runs three tests.

Test one fires that story directly at the part of you bracing for it. Full speed. Your body reacts — flinch, brace, dread. Then you check: did it actually hit anything? When you look — nothing there. The bullets were real. No target. Your nervous system updates on the spot. The fear drops.

Test two holds both forces at the same time — the drive to fix it and the dread underneath. Not choosing either one. When both are held equally, the movement that was generating the tension has nowhere to go. It stops on its own.

Test three looks directly at what’s left — the one central sensation. You point at it and check: does it have edges? Where does it begin? Is it big or small? Every answer breaks down. What the tension was gripping was never there. A cloud gripping sky.

Each test is experiential — you run it and your body reports back. You either feel the result or you don’t. Nothing to believe. Nothing to practice. Just three checks that take about twenty minutes.

The situation that looked unsurvivable becomes just a situation. The next move becomes obvious.

The film will try to run again. When it does, you come back and run the tests again. Each time, the grip loosens.
